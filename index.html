Why Get Braces?

Braces do more than straighten teeth. They help fix your bite. They guide your jaw into the right spot. They can even change the way your face looks—for the better.

A straighter smile is also easier to clean. That means healthier gums and fewer cavities.

Kids aren't the only ones getting braces anymore. More adults are going for it now too. Some want to fix long-standing issues. Others just want to feel more confident about their smile.

Most Popular Braces Options at Cypress Dental Clinics

Cypress dental clinics have something for everyone. Here are the most common types of braces you'll come across:

Traditional Metal Braces

These are the ones you probably picture when someone says "braces." Metal brackets and wires slowly move your teeth into place.

Why people pick them:

  • They work great for tough cases
  • Cost-effective
  • Tough and long-lasting

Best for: Kids and teens who need a full fix.

Ceramic Braces

Ceramic braces do the same job as metal ones. The difference? The brackets match your teeth, so they're harder to spot.

Good things about them:

  • Blend in well
  • Look nicer in photos
  • Popular with older teens and adults

Keep in mind: You'll need to clean them well to avoid stains.

Lingual Braces

These go behind your teeth. They're hidden from sight but still work just like regular braces.

Why people like them:

  • Can't see them when you smile
  • Made to fit your teeth exactly
  • Super private option

Things to know: They might feel weird at first. Also, they usually cost more.

Clear Aligners (Invisalign and similar brands)

Clear aligners are a modern choice. They're smooth, plastic trays that gently move your teeth.

Why people love them:

  • Easy to take off for meals and brushing
  • Nearly invisible
  • No metal, no wires

Perfect for: Adults and teens with small to medium alignment problems.

Braces Before and After: What Really Happens?

Those amazing braces before and after photos didn't happen overnight. Here's what the whole journey looks like:

First Visit

It all starts with a check-up and X-rays at a Cypress dental clinic. The dentist will tell you which type of braces fits your case.

Getting Them On

Once you choose, they put the braces on. It doesn't hurt, but your mouth might feel a little tight.

Monthly Adjustments

Every few weeks, you'll go back to get wires tightened or trays updated. This keeps your teeth moving in the right direction.

Braces Off

After one to two years, the braces come off. And there it is—a straight, clean, confident smile.

Time for Retainers

To keep that new smile in place, you'll wear a retainer. Sometimes it's removable. Other times it's fixed behind your teeth.

FAQs

Q: How long do I need to wear braces?

Most people wear them for 12 to 24 months. It depends on your teeth and your treatment plan.

Q: Can I get braces if I'm an adult?

Yes, adults in Cypress get braces all the time. Many prefer clear aligners or ceramic options.

Q: Are clear aligners as good as braces?

They work well for mild to moderate cases. Your dentist will let you know if they're a fit.

Q: Will braces hurt?

You might feel sore for a few days after adjustments. But the pain goes away quickly.

Q: What foods should I avoid with braces?

Stay away from sticky, hard, or chewy stuff. It can break brackets or wires.
